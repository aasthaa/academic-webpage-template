Guest Lecturer

Department of Biostatistics, University of Washington
  • 2011: Statistical Methods for the Evaluation of Biomarkers and Medical Tests (Biost 578)
    Graduate level special topics course for biostatistics students. Presents concepts and techniques necessary for evaluating biomarkers or other medical tests used for classification or prediction purposes. This includes applications in diagnosis, disease screening, prognosis and risk prediction.

Teaching Assistant

Department of Biostatistics, University of Washington
  • 2010: Biostatistical Reasoning for the Health Sciences (Biost 508)
    Graduate level course for non-biostatistics students. Provides a broad overview of biostatistical methods, emphasizing interpretation and concepts rather than computation or mathematical details. Introduces basic concepts of study design, data summaries and presentation, statistical inference (including hypothesis testing, p-values, and confidence intervals) and modeling approaches such as regression analysis.
  • 2009: Medical Biometry II (Biost 512)
    Graduate level course for non-biostatistics students. Topics include multiple regression, analysis of covariance, and an introduction to one-way and two-way analyses of variance, including assumptions, transformations, outlier detection, dummy variables, and variable selection procedures. Examples drawn from the biomedical literature with computer assignments using standard statistical computer packages.
